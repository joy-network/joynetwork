Writing a Tech CV That Opens Doors

Insights & Sharing · Tech Careers · Updated 2026

One of the biggest pain points for tech professionals today is having strong skills but a CV that fails to get noticed.

Recruiters and hiring managers are not ignoring experience — they simply cannot see the impact behind it. A strong CV is not about listing everything that has been done. It is about making value unmistakably clear.

1️⃣ Lead with outcomes

Avoid describing tasks alone. Focus on why the work mattered and what it changed.

  • “Built automation scripts” → “Automated reporting workflows, saving 10+ hours per week.”
  • “Developed an internal tool” → “Built an internal tool that reduced data processing time by 45%.”

2️⃣ Speak the company’s language

Each role values different tools, outcomes, and priorities. Tailor the CV so the hiring team can immediately see alignment with the role and the business needs.

3️⃣ Keep it clean and human

Readable design, short bullet points, consistent formatting, and clear section headers make a CV easier to scan.

A short summary helps highlight who the candidate is — not just what they do.

“Backend engineer passionate about building scalable systems that make data flow effortlessly.”

4️⃣ Add a professional summary

Start with a concise 2–3 line overview that highlights core expertise and what drives the work. This helps hiring managers connect beyond the tech stack.

5️⃣ Quantify impact

Numbers attract attention. They demonstrate credibility and show how work translates into real business results.

A CV is more than a document — it is a professional story. It should reflect not only what has been done, but the difference it has made.

A strong CV should feel like a well-built product: structured, clear, and designed to perform. In a competitive market, clarity is a powerful advantage.
